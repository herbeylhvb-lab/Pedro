Where Pedro Stands

The priorities driving the work at City Hall for District 4.

  • Infrastructure That Lasts

    Streets, drainage, and utilities that hold up to South Texas weather and growing demand. The flagship example is the $14 million Old Highway 77 reconstruction — a complete rebuild with new curbs and gutters, upgraded drainage, sidewalks, and dedicated bike trails, with sewer and stormwater lines installed at average depths of fifteen feet. The work is on track to wrap up in Fall 2026. Pedro keeps pushing for projects like this that fix the roads people actually use every day.

  • Public Safety

    Safer neighborhoods start with well-resourced first responders and active community partnerships. Pedro supports giving police, fire, and EMS the tools they need while building trust between city services and the residents they serve.

  • Small Business & Economic Opportunity

    Brownsville grows when small businesses grow. Pedro advocates for policies that help local entrepreneurs succeed, from streamlined permitting to targeted support programs for neighborhood commercial corridors.

  • Affordable, Reliable Services

    Water, power, and trash service that residents can count on without breaking the family budget. Pedro focuses on holding rates in check and making sure essential services are delivered consistently across every District 4 neighborhood.

  • Transparent, Responsive Government

    Residents deserve to know how decisions are made and to have real access to their Commissioner. Pedro prioritizes open communication, community meetings, and timely responses to District 4 concerns.
