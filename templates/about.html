About Pedro Cardenas

City Commissioner, District 4

Pedro E. Cardenas serves the residents of District 4 on the Brownsville City Commission. First elected in June 2021 and re-elected in June 2025, he brings a background in business administration and account management to his work on the Commission.

Pedro holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from Tecnológico de Monterrey (2003). Before entering public service, he built a career in business and account management, experience that shapes his focus on fiscal responsibility and practical results at City Hall.

As Commissioner, Pedro has championed infrastructure investment, including the $14 million reconstruction of Old Highway 77 — a project that replaces the roadway and adds upgraded drainage, new curbs and gutters, sidewalks, and dedicated bike trails, with sewer and stormwater lines installed at average depths of fifteen feet. Completion is expected in Fall 2026. Pedro has described the project as “a major investment to improve roadway safety, drainage and utility services for families and businesses.”

Beyond Old Highway 77, Pedro has worked to strengthen public safety, support local small businesses, and keep essential services — water, power, streets, and trash collection — affordable and reliable for District 4 families. He regularly holds community meetings and maintains active channels on social media so constituents can reach him directly.

His current term runs through May 2029.
